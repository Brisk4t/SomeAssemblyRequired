article Truly Yours

3D printed parts mean infinite personalization.

Not all bodies are the same, and neither are all rehabilitation journeys. The 3D printed design allows for infinite personalization, and the modular architecture means new parts can be added as needs evolve.

Therapists can prescribe custom attachments and routines, and patients can feel empowered to take ownership of their rehabilitation by tweaking the design or printing new parts as they progress.

article Everyone's Invited

Current DIY furniture designs are optimized for ease of assembly, but they assume full two-handed mobility.

Putting together flat-pack furniture can be a frustrating experience for anyone, but for individuals with upper limb impairments, the standard designs can be completely inaccessible. By rethinking the design with accessibility in mind, we can create products that are not only easier to assemble for everyone, but also open up new possibilities for independence and empowerment for those with mobility challenges.

This doesn't have to be the case. Traditional fixtures have simpler alternatives that can be used by anyone, regardless of their physical abilities.
